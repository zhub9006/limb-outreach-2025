## 🔑 Key Trials of Interest

### 1. VA/KSH — Variable-Compliance Socket System for Transradial Amputees
- **NCT ID**: [NCT05990062](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05990062)
- **Status**: 🟢 **RECRUITING**
- **Sponsor**: VA Office of Research and Development + Stumpworx LLC
- **Focus**: Affordable, breathable prosthetic arm socket using athletic shoe technology (textile + polymer hybrid)
- **Why It Matters**: Addresses the #1 reason for prosthetic abandonment — socket discomfort and poor fit. Uses cost-effective materials (nylon FDM/FFF, textile patterns) instead of expensive custom silicone.
- **Phases**: 3-phase study (design → validation → broad testing)
- **Population**: Veterans with transradial amputations
- **Estimated Completion**: Sept 2028

### 2. C-Leg 3 & C-Leg 4 — Microprocessor Controlled Knee
- **NCT ID**: [NCT02765035](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT02765035)
- **Status**: ⛔ **COMPLETED**
- **Sponsor**: Otto Bock Healthcare Products GmbH (Industry)
- **Focus**: Comparing microprocessor-controlled vs. mechanical knee joints for affordability and function
- **Why It Matters**: Microprocessor knees improve mobility but are expensive; this study provides data to justify cost vs. benefit
- **Population**: 18 transfemoral amputees
- **Key Outcomes**: 6-min gait test, stairs/hill assessment, balance confidence

### 3. University of Bath — Personalisation of Prosthetic Care
- **NCT ID**: [NCT06243549](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06243549)
- **Status**: 🟡 **ACTIVE NOT RECRUITING**
- **Sponsor**: University of Bath + NHS Trusts (UK)
- **Focus**: Understanding biomechanical/psychological pathways of lower back pain post-amputation to inform cost-effective care programs
- **Why It Matters**: Personalized care reduces unnecessary interventions and long-term costs
- **Population**: 30 unilateral lower-limb amputees
- **Estimated Completion**: Oct 2026

## 🔍 Additional Recommended Searches

These are active areas where low-cost prosthetic innovation is needed:

1. **3D-Printed Prosthetics** — Search for trials on additive-manufactured prostheses (lower cost, customizable)
2. **Body-Powered Prosthetics** — Historically more affordable than myoelectric; trials on improving comfort
3. **Prosthetic Training & Rehabilitation** — Cost-effective rehab protocols to reduce abandonment
4. **Developing Country Prosthetics** — Trials focused on accessible, low-cost solutions for underserved populations
5. **Socket Technology Alternatives** — Textile-based, breathable sockets to reduce skin complications
